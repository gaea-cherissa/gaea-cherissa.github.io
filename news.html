January 30, 2021 : Fairbanks Arts Association's F.A.M. Show



The Fairbanks Art Association’s member invitational show this past month was good time – a delightful turnout of community members!


This painting I contributed to the show depicts an aerial view of Healy and the Usibelli Coal Mine – the only operational coal mine in Alaska. The area is a open-pit surface mine operation. Surface mining involves accessing coal (or other minerals) by clearing forests and using heavy earth-moving equipment and explosives to excavate the earth to access coal seams that are up to a few hundred feet below the surface.


Coal mined from Usibelli fuels 6 coal-fired power plants which provide about 40% of the electricity in Interior Alaska.


Included in these 6 coal-fired power plants is the Fort Wainwright 65-year old central heat and power plant (CHPP), which is at risk of malfunction or failure and presently under evaluation to remain in use, be replaced by a new coal-fired CHPP, be replaced by a natural gas-fueled system, or potentially other proposed alternatives that meet their criteria.


A Draft Environmental Impact Statement regarding this decision is open for a second round of public comments until February 22, 2021.
